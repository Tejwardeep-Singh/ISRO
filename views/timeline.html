Timeline of ISRO

Explore the Journey of ISRO

Discover the milestones and achievements of the Indian Space Research Organisation through our interactive timeline.

1969

Foundation of ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation was established on August 15, 1969, with the vision to harness space technology for national development.

1975

Launch of Aryabhata

Aryabhata, India's first satellite, was launched on April 19, 1975, marking a significant milestone in India's space journey.

1980

Launch of Rohini Satellite

The Rohini satellite was launched on July 18, 1980, using the SLV-3 rocket, making India the sixth country to develop its own launch vehicle.

1994

Launch of IRS-1A

The Indian Remote Sensing (IRS) program was initiated with the launch of IRS-1A on September 7, 1982, enhancing India's capabilities in earth observation.

2008

Chandrayaan-1 Mission

Chandrayaan-1, India's first lunar probe, was launched on October 22, 2008, and it made significant discoveries about the Moon's surface.

2013

Mangalyaan Mission

India's Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) was launched on November 5, 2013, making India the first Asian nation to reach Martian orbit.

2020

Chandrayaan-2 Mission

Chandrayaan-2, India's second lunar exploration mission, was launched on July 22, 2019, with an orbiter, lander, and rover.

2023

Aditya-L1 Mission

Aditya-L1, India's first solar mission, was launched on September 2, 2023, to study the Sun's outer layers and its impact on space weather.

2024

Gaganyaan Mission

Gaganyaan, India's first crewed space mission, is scheduled for launch in 2024, aiming to send Indian astronauts into space.
